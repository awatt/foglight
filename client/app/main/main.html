PHARMALENS

The worldwide market for Diabetes Mellitus (DM) pharmaceuticals is on track to reach 1 billion people by 2035, including those with DM and those at high risk for the disease. With 29.1 million Americans (9.3%) currently suffering from DM and an additional 86 million (37%) over the age of 20 with prediabetes, the U.S. will continue to be one of the most lucrative markets for DM-related pharmaceuticals.

Recent years have seen a massive influx of big pharma marketing payments to prescribing physicians to boost sales of DM-related drugs. These payments took the form of, among other things, meals, drug samples, and fees for speaking at medical conferences and teaching Continuing Medical Education (CME) courses to other practitioners.
